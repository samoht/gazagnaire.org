Thomas Gazagnaire

I am a Senior Research Associate at the University of Cambridge. Before that, I was Chief Technical Officier at OCamlPro (where I designed and lead the implementation of OPAM) and Principal Virtualisation Engineer at OnApp.

I have also been working as a postdoctoral fellow at INRIA sophia-antipolis, in the INDES project and as a Senior Software Engineer at Citrix, working on the XenServer and Xen Cloud Platform softwares. I completed my Ph.D in 2008 at IRISA in the DistribCom project. I obtained my Master and Bachelor degrees from ENS Lyon and ENS Cachan-Bretagne.

Research

My research interests primarily involve the application of formal methods and functional programming languages to complex-system engineering problems. More generally, I have a wide range of research and application interests including:

  • Cloud-computing;
  • Distributed and concurrent systems;
  • Verification (model-checking) of complex systems;
  • Automatic fault-diagnosis and monitoring of complex systems;
  • Application of functional programming to real-world problems;
  • Compilation and optimization techniques;
  • Partial-order theory.

I am doing most of my software developments in OCaml. My projects are hosted on github.
